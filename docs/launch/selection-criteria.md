# Beta User Selection Criteria

Use these criteria to keep the first cohort small, constructive, and operationally manageable.

## Accept Immediately

- Has at least 1 year of crypto trading or simulation experience
- Can describe bugs or confusion clearly
- Is willing to provide active feedback during the beta window
- Understands that the product is simulation-first and advisory-only
- Aligns with the target operating systems under test

## Good Waitlist Candidates

- Beginner users who are thoughtful but may need more onboarding support
- Users with limited weekly availability
- Passive feedback contributors
- Testers from oversubscribed operating-system groups

## Reject For This Round

- Expects guaranteed profits or automated returns
- Refuses the disclaimer or risk acknowledgment
- Shows clear confusion about non-custodial or simulation-first boundaries
- Cannot provide a valid communication channel for follow-up
- Has a history of toxic or disruptive community behavior

## Cohort Balance Targets

- 20 to 50 testers total
- Healthy mix of English and Arabic users
- Windows-first coverage, with at least a few macOS and Linux testers
- Blend of intermediate and advanced users with a small number of beginners

## Operational Notes

- Prefer clarity and reliability over audience size
- Keep a waitlist from day one
- Record why someone was accepted or delayed
- Do not over-index on follower count or social reach
