# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want CryptoDesk AI to be a respectful, constructive, and security-minded project. As contributors and maintainers, we pledge to make participation in this community harassment-free for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to acting and interacting in ways that build an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- showing empathy and patience
- giving and receiving constructive feedback
- assuming good intent while still being honest about technical risks
- respecting boundaries around security, privacy, and responsible disclosure
- focusing on what is best for the project and its users

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- insulting, demeaning, or inflammatory comments
- publishing private information without explicit permission
- bad-faith security disclosures or attempts to expose secrets
- any behavior that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is officially representing the project or its community.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it through one of these channels:

- GitHub issues for non-sensitive moderation concerns
- the repository security policy for sensitive disclosures
- direct contact with the maintainer when privacy is important

All reports will be reviewed and handled fairly and promptly.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate, including warning a contributor, temporarily restricting participation, or removing access from project spaces.
